## Tracking Rules

For any overlapping report period:

1. Build an objective evidence map from the worklog rows in the report period.
2. For each objective, summarize recent evidence, likely KR coverage, missing evidence, blockers, and next checkpoint.
3. Separate evidence strength:
   - `strong`: explicit objective/KR/repo/project name or direct benchmark/SDK/Code-as-Spreadsheet/Agent-Builder evidence
   - `medium`: related repo or project wording but no explicit KR
   - `weak`: inferred only from generic terms; use as a warning, not proof
4. Do not invent progress percentages. Use qualitative status unless the logs contain explicit measured results.
5. Keep reports honest: a quiet objective is a warning, not success.

## Risk And Warning Heuristics

Surface these signals in personal and team reports:

- `No evidence`: P0 objective has no mapped worklog evidence in the report period.
- `Owner gap`: expected owner has no update during the period, or the member is missing from `People`.
- `Blocked`: mapped rows have `状态=阻塞`, non-empty `阻塞`, or explicit blocked wording.
- `Risk`: mapped rows have non-empty `风险` or risk wording.
- `Cadence risk`: O4 has no strong evidence by the end of week 1; O1/O2/O3 have no strong evidence for 3 consecutive working days.
- `Acceptance gap`: work mentions activity but not acceptance proof such as official ranking, reusable platform, migration coverage, MVP closure, diff/replay demo, capability matrix, regression pass, formula Rust compatibility/correctness proof plus 10x benchmark evidence, docs export verification, or Slide MVP dogfood evidence.
- `Unmapped effort`: many high-priority rows do not map to any objective; flag as possible focus drift.
- `Stale milestone`: a KR appears repeatedly as planned next step without completed evidence.
